I Am Learning to Code!

I Am Terrified of All of This

There is, at least for me, a certain level of anxiety to coding. I feel super cool typing loudly and entering snippets of code but it is also quite stressful at the same time.
I am very talented at dragging and dropping things into buckets on the super user friendly websites such as Wix.com, but typing a bunch of stuff into a solid screen and hoping it turns out well is very, very scary.

On the other hand, when I can sucessfully turn a bunch of words and symbols into a webpage that actually loads in a mainstream browser, I feel like:

  • A wizard
  • A genie
  • Taylor Swift

I mean, Taylor Swift has got to feel like she is making magic when she rattles off a song. I heard her on a talk show once saying that she can create a song in, maybe, half an hour.
That's gotta be magic. That's gotta be actual magic.

I Do Not Intend to Give Up

I am prone, especially when frustrated, to bouts of crying. But that's okay. I know that it releases stress, so I'll just cry and work.
At least it's fall so I can have windows open and a cool breeze will blow across my face while I cry. Whoops, this is Kansas where it seems to be in the nineties until October.

I like to tell my children, my students, and myself that we can all do hard things. We just need to persevere, to keep trying. So what I must do, also, is to persevere and just keep trying new things until I have a website that makes me proud.

Sometimes, when I feel stressed out I look at trees, or imagine that I am out in the wilderness. Perhaps I am on a hike in this visualization (obviously this fictional hike happens on a cool day with moderate cloud cover - sweaty hikes are not my preferred type of hike). After I have spent a moment calming myself through visualization, I refer to my text book and my notes, and I try again!
